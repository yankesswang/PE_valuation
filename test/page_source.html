Amazon.com PE Ratio Formula

How is AMZN's pe ratio calculated?
PE Ratio = Share Price / Diluted EPS (TTM)
= $235.42 / $4.67
= 50.41
Defining pe ratio:
The price to earnings ratio is a very popular financial ratio used to help determine how expensive or cheap a stock currently is. It's determined by dividing the current share price by ttm earnings per share (or market cap / ttm net income).
There's no 'good' pe ratio to look for. A company's pe ratio is determined by many factors including the sector/industry it's in and how fast it's growing. A pe ratio that's trending downward indicates a company is getting 'cheaper' while a pe ratio that's trending upward indicates a company is getting more 'expensive'. If you notice a company's pe ratio is lower than average and you believe its future prospects are bright it might be a good time to buy.
Defining forward pe ratio:
The forward pe ratio is an estimate based on analysts' projected earnings for the next 12 months. Treat it as skeptically as you would any future estimate. Past numbers are real, the future is unknown.
Expanded definitions: Investopedia, Wikipedia, Corporate Finance Institute, Wall Street Prep
